About

In 2013, Saint Mary's changed the format of its fourteen year old high school programming competition to a week long development competition. The hack-a-week was very successful with participation from more than 30 high school students and half a dozen industry sponsors.

In 2015, we're at it again, this time opening to students at all levels aswell as the general public. If you fit into one of these categories, you're in!

  • High School Student
  • University Student
  • Open Category Participant
We strongly encourage collaborations between the categories and there will be special prizes for hybrid teams. Teams can be re-configured on May 8th to include up to four members.

Frequently Asked

Hack-a-Week?
Based on emerging hackathon culture the Hack-A-Week is a platform for students of all paths to get together and learn from each other. You can find out more about hackathons on Wikipedia

Languages?
We encourage the use of any language or platform without restriction. Want an extra challenge? Do your hack in a new language or platform.

Problems?
We will inspire you with sample problems to do, however there is no restriction if you have something else in mind.
